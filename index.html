About

Saba is a multidisciplinary artist originally from Pakistan who has been based in Paris for over a decade. During her formative years in Hong Kong, she completely immersed herself in the arts—playing musical instruments, singing in choirs, and acting in theatrical productions. A lifelong creative, Saba has been painting and drawing since childhood. Her early fascination with performance was sparked by annual school plays, where she took on diverse roles ranging from a Troll to Felicity Morgan in Robert Swindells' Room 13, cementing her lifelong passion for acting.

Holding a Bachelor’s degree in Graphic Design (majoring in UX/UI) from LISAA Paris and a diverse professional background, she continually pushes her artistic boundaries through filmmaking and poetry. Alongside her growing interest in photography, Saba remains dedicated to painting and sketching, specialising in emotive portraits that capture frequently stigmatised or "shunned" emotions.
